Founder & Organizer, Rutgers Math Circle:

Rutgers Math Circle is a math outreach program with a target audience of K-12 students, primarily those in middle school and high school. We host sessions in Hill Center every other Thursday evening, where a faculty member or graduate student gives an interactive talk on a math topic that is not typically covered in the K-12 curriculum. In addition, undergraduate volunteers help out at every session, facilitating discussion among the younger students. Some sample talks have discussed minimal surfaces using bubbles, the mathematics behind games, and graph theory.

If you're interested in getting involved with Rutgers Math Circle as a participant, speaker, or volunteer, please visit our website and reach out to rutgersmathcircle@gmail.com with any questions!

Faculty, BEAM Summer Away:

BEAM Summer Away is a three-week intensive math summer camp for rising eighth graders. Here, students are exposed to proof-based mathematics and are encouraged to develop their mathematical confidence and independence. As faculty at BEAM Summer Away, I develop and teach a three-week-long course on mathematical problem solving.
