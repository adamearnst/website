About Me

My name is Adam, and I'm a Ph.D. Candidate in Mathematics at Rutgers University-New Brunswick under the advisement of Dr. Matthew P. Young. My research interests include analytic number theory, automorphic forms, and L-functions. Before coming to New Jersey, I graduated from the University of Michigan with majors in Mathematics and Interdisciplinary Physics.

I am very passionate about math outreach. I organize Rutgers Math Circle, an outreach program designed to expose K-12 students to math outside the standard curriculum. See Outreach for a full list of my outreach activities.

Outside of mathematics, I play the cello and love listening to classical music. I'm also an avid birder, and I've recently started to explore wildlife photography.
